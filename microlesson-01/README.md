## Scenario

You're an analyst on a product team exploring a new pricing tier or feature bundle. You have a collection of competitor listings, support chats, and user reviews. 

**Your challenge:** Use GenAI to identify market gaps, define customer personas, and ideate actionable product opportunities.

---

## Lab Objectives

By the end of this lab, you will:

- Use GenAI to surface *market gaps* from mixed-source product data.
- Generate customer personas using the language and pain points from real users.
- Propose new product features or bundles matched to specific personas.
- Evaluate GenAI’s output for business relevance and reliability.

---

## Getting Started

**Lab files:**
- [competitor_product_listings.txt](assets/competitor_product_listings.txt)
- [customer_reviews.csv](assets/customer_reviews.csv)
- [support_chat_excerpts.txt](assets/support_chat_excerpts.txt)

---

## Step 1: Explore the Data

1. **Scan the sources:**  
   - Read through the competitor listings, customer reviews, and support chats.
   - Jot down initial impressions—what jumps out? Any patterns, pain points, or missing features?

2. **Choose your data focus:**  
   - Pick **two** sources to use in your first GenAI prompt (e.g., reviews + competitor listings).

---

## Step 2: Prompt GenAI for Market Gaps

1. **Draft your prompt:**  
   - Clearly state the data context and your analysis goal.
   - Example:  
     > “Here are excerpts from competitor listings and recent user reviews. What product needs or features appear to be missing from the market?”

2. **Submit to GenAI:**  
   - Paste your data (summarized or full excerpts) and your prompt into your GenAI tool (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, etc.).

3. **Review the output:**  
   - What gaps did the AI spot?  
   - Are they truly “white space” or just restatements of complaints?

---

## Step 3: Persona Creation

1. **Prompt GenAI to generate personas:**  
   - Use qualitative language from reviews/support chats.
   - Example:  
     > “Based on these user reviews and support chats, create 2–3 customer personas. Use the actual language from users where possible. What are their main needs, goals, and pain points?”

2. **Evaluate:**  
   - Do the personas feel realistic and specific?
   - Where does GenAI generalize or make unsupported assumptions?

---

## Step 4: Ideate Product Opportunities

1. **Prompt GenAI for ideas:**  
   - Example:  
     > “Suggest 2 new product ideas or feature bundles that directly address the needs of the personas you created. Explain how each would benefit a specific persona.”

2. **Check the results:**  
   - Are ideas actionable and relevant?
   - Which would you actually pitch to a stakeholder?

---

## Step 5: Reflection & Real-World Application

Reflect on your workflow:

- What did GenAI surface that you missed?
- Where was human judgment most important?
- How would you edit the prompts for better results?
- How might you reframe the output for an executive audience?

---

## Wrap-Up & Peer Debrief

- Share your personas and product ideas with peers.
- Discuss:

  - What felt generic?  
  - What prompt tweaks led to better business outcomes?
